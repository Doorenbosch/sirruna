Masthead Brand Options

Current Execution
The Litmus Daily

Issues: Too long, over-spaced (0.15em), feels generic. Wide tracking was a 2010s luxury trend that's now dated.

Option A — Mixed Case, Tight Recommended
The Litmus

Why it works: Mixed case is modern yet authoritative. Tight tracking (0.01em) creates density and impact. "The" adds gravitas like "The Economist". Burgundy maintains brand identity. Drop "Daily"—your readers know it's daily.

Option B — Single Word, Bold
Litmus

Why it works: Maximum impact with minimum characters. Like Bloomberg, Reuters, Axios. The single word becomes the brand. Slightly wider tracking (0.04em) works here because the word is short.

Option C — Slash Incorporated
L/tmus

Why it works: The "/" from your logo becomes part of the wordmark. Distinctive, ownable, memorable. Creates visual consistency between icon and name. Nobody else has this.

Option D — Playfair Display
The Litmus

Why it works: Playfair has more personality and contrast than Source Serif. Slightly editorial, slightly modern. Negative tracking (-0.01em) creates cohesion.

Option E — Black, Minimal
Litmus

Why it works: Let the logo carry the burgundy brand color. Black text is classic newspaper—FT, WSJ, NYT all use black mastheads. Creates contrast with the colorful logo.

Option F — FT Style (Libre Baskerville)
The Litmus

Why it works: Libre Baskerville is closer to the actual FT masthead font. Zero tracking, black ink, serious journalism vibes. Most traditional option.

Option G — Cormorant Garamond
Litmus

Why it works: Elegant, refined, high-contrast. Larger size works because the letterforms are thinner. Premium feel without being stuffy. One word keeps it clean.
